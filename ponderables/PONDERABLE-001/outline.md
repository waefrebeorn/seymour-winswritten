## Outline

### Opening Scene — The Copying Animal
A fictional prehistoric scene. A hominid watches another make a tool. Copies it. Passes it on. The first meme.

### Chapter 1: What Separates Us
- Tool use is not unique. Crows use tools. Otters use tools.
- What's unique: imitation. High-fidelity cultural transmission.
- The saddleback bird parallel (P.F. Jenkins, New Zealand) — learned songs mutate across generations
- Humans: the only species that copies with intention

REF: [P.F. Jenkins, saddleback bird song cultural mutation]

### Chapter 2: Dawkins' Error
- Dawkins was right about memes. Wrong about the timescale.
- He thought memes would evolve slowly, like genes.
- He didn't predict the internet.

QUOTE: [Richard Dawkins, The Selfish Gene, Ch. 11]

### Chapter 3: The Meme Pool
- Pre-internet: memes had a natural death rate. Forgetting was a feature.
- Oral tradition: each retelling was a mutation.
- Print: higher fidelity, but still lossy.
- The internet changed the death rate to zero.

QUOTE: [Colonel: "trivial information is accumulating every second, preserved in all its triteness"]

### Chapter 4: The 90s Artifact
- Clipart CD-ROMs: 200,000 images, all equally accessible, none curated.
- This was the last era of physical visual culture.
- The aesthetic of abundance: everything available, nothing selected.
- Why Seymour uses 90s clipart: it's the visual language of pre-curation.

ART: [Request: 90s clipart of a person at a computer, surrounded by floating images]

### Chapter 5: The S3 Protocol
- MGS2 Colonel: "Selection for Societal Sanity"
- The Patriots' argument: too much information slows evolution.
- Seymour's counter-argument: curation is not control. Context is not censorship.
- The Ponderable as memetic filter.

QUOTE: [Colonel: "What we propose to do is not to control content, but to create context."]

QUOTE: [Colonel: "Just as in genetics, unnecessary information and memory must be filtered out to stimulate the evolution of the species."]

### Chapter 6: The Future Archaeologist
- A fictional scene: a person in a post-digital world finds a Seymour Ponderable in a physical library.
- It's the only surviving record of a meme that once infected billions.
- The book doesn't explain the meme. It creates context around it.

QUOTE: [Raiden: "I'll pick my own name, and my own life. I'll find something worth passing on."]

### Closing: The Seymour Thesis
Every meme wants to live forever. Most don't deserve to.

But some do. And those are the ones Seymour selects.
