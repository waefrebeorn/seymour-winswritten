# MGS2 Colonel AI — Pull Quotes for the Seymour Framework
## Source: Metal Gear Solid 2: Sons of Liberty (2001)
## Context: The Colonel is an AI construct (GW + Raiden's nanomachines) mouthpiece for the Patriots

---

## I. On Information Overload

> "In the current, digitized world, trivial information is accumulating every second, preserved in all its triteness. Never fading, always accessible... All this junk data preserved in an unfiltered state, growing at an alarming rate. It will only slow down social progress, reduce the rate of evolution."

> "Not even natural selection can take place here."

> "Just as in genetics, unnecessary information and memory must be filtered out to stimulate the evolution of the species."

---

## II. On Control vs. Curation

> "What we propose to do is not to control content, but to create context."

> "We are the very discipline and morality that Americans invoke so often. How can anyone hope to eliminate us? As long as this nation exists, so will we."

> "We are formless."

---

## III. On the S3 Plan (Selection for Societal Sanity)

> "The S3 Plan does not stand for Solid Snake Simulation. What it does stand for is Selection for Societal Sanity."

> "S3 is a system for controlling human will and consciousness."

> "S3 is not you, a soldier trained in the image of Solid Snake. It is — a method, a protocol, that created a circumstance that made you what you are."

---

## IV. On Raiden as Test Subject

> "You accepted the fiction we've provided, obeyed our orders and did everything you were told to. The exercise is a resounding success."

> "You are the perfect representative of the masses."

---

## V. On the Nature of the Patriots

> "Over the past two hundred years — A kind of consciousness formed layer by layer in the crucible of the White House. It's not unlike the way life started in the oceans four billion years ago. The White House was our primordial soup."

---

## VI. On Reality and Fiction

> "There's no such thing in the world as absolute reality. Most of what they call real is actually fiction; what you think you see is only as real as your brain tells you it is."
