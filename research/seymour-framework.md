# Seymour Project — Research & Framework

## Meme Etymology & Academic Foundations

### Origin
- **Term coined:** Richard Dawkins, _The Selfish Gene_ (1976), Chapter 11: "Memes: The New Replicators"
- **Etymology:** Greek _mimēma_ (μίμημα) = "imitated thing" → from _mimeisthai_ = "to imitate" → from _mimos_ = "mime"
- **Dawkins' definition:** A unit of cultural transmission, analogous to the gene in biological evolution

### Key Concepts
| Concept | Definition |
|---------|------------|
| **Meme** | Unit of cultural information spread by imitation |
| **Memeplex** | Co-adapted group of memes (e.g., religions, languages, scientific theories) |
| **Cultural mutation** | Slight modification of learned behavior across generations (cf. saddleback bird songs, P.F. Jenkins) |
| **Selfish replicator** | Memes that propagate for their own replication, not necessarily for host benefit |
| **Viruses of the mind** | Memes that replicate effectively despite being detrimental to hosts (Dawkins on religion) |

### Memetics Timeline
| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1976 | Dawkins coins "meme" in _The Selfish Gene_ |
| 1983 | _Meme_ added to Oxford English Dictionary (draft) |
| 1996 | _Virus of the Mind_ by Richard Brodie popularizes memetics |
| 1999 | Susan Blackmore's _The Meme Machine_ — full scientific theory of memes |
| 2001 | MGS2: Sons of Liberty — S3 Plan, information control as theme |
| 2005+ | Internet memes emerge as dominant cultural form |
| 2010s | Memetics criticized as unfalsifiable; field fragments |
| 2020s | AI-generated content creates new meme ecology; "meme" fully divorced from Dawkins' original meaning |
| 2026 | Seymour publishes first Ponderables — reclaiming memetics through allegory |

### Seymour's Thesis
The internet completed the process MGS2 predicted: total information preservation with no natural selection. Every tweet, every post, every meme preserved in amber. Seymour's Ponderables are the **anti-algorithm** — human-curated, physically printed, deliberately sequenced. Each book is a memeplex. Each Ponderable is a meme given context, history, and allegorical weight.

The 90s clipart aesthetic is intentional: it's the visual language of the last era before digital saturation. Pre-internet visual culture, preserved and repurposed.
